<div id="electoralism" class="content-section">
  <h2>Electoralism</h2>
  <p>Electoral politics is one tool among many. Used strategically, voting can reduce harm and create openings for deeper change—but it should never be mistaken for the whole of political action.</p>

  <div class="action-grid">
    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#128269;</div>
      <h4>Research Your Area & Zoning</h4>
      <p>Understanding local political geography is essential for effective electoral strategy. Know your districts, voting history, and where progressive candidates can win.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Use <strong>Dave's Redistricting App</strong> to understand your district boundaries and demographics</li>
          <li>Review <strong>Ballotpedia</strong> for local election results and upcoming races</li>
          <li>Check your county's election website for historical precinct-level data</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://www.ballotpedia.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ballotpedia</a> — Comprehensive election information</li>
          <li><a href="https://davesredistricting.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Dave's Redistricting</a> — District mapping tools</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#9878;</div>
      <h4>Vote for Harm Reduction in Swing Areas</h4>
      <p>In competitive districts, strategic voting can prevent the worst outcomes. This doesn't mean uncritical support—it means recognizing material differences while organizing for better options.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Identify swing races using <strong>Cook Political Report</strong> ratings</li>
          <li>Focus volunteer energy on close races where your effort has maximum impact</li>
          <li>Join <strong>Swing Left</strong> or similar organizations for coordinated action</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://swingleft.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Swing Left</a> — Coordinated swing district organizing</li>
          <li><a href="https://cookpolitical.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cook Political Report</a> — Race ratings and analysis</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#9994;</div>
      <h4>Support Pro-Worker Candidates Everywhere</h4>
      <p>In non-competitive districts, support genuinely pro-worker candidates to shift the Overton window and build a bench of progressive leaders for future races.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Support candidates endorsed by <strong>Democratic Socialists of America (DSA)</strong></li>
          <li>Check endorsements from <strong>Working Families Party</strong> for pro-labor candidates</li>
          <li>Look for candidates with union endorsements, especially from militant unions like <strong>UNITE HERE</strong> or <strong>National Nurses United</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://www.dsausa.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">DSA Endorsements</a> — Democratic Socialists of America</li>
          <li><a href="https://workingfamilies.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Working Families Party</a> — Pro-worker electoral organizing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div id="unionization" class="content-section">
  <h2>Unionization</h2>
  <p>Unions are the most effective tool workers have for improving their conditions. Collective bargaining power transforms individual grievances into collective strength. Every workplace can be organized.</p>

  <div class="highlight-box">
    <h4>Why Unions Matter</h4>
    <p>Union workers earn <strong>10-20% more</strong> than non-union workers in similar jobs. Beyond wages, unions provide job security, safer working conditions, and a voice in workplace decisions. Historically, strong unions correlated with lower inequality and stronger middle classes.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="action-grid">
    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#128101;</div>
      <h4>Join an Existing Union</h4>
      <p>If your workplace already has union representation, become an active member. If not, check if unions in your industry are organizing nearby workplaces.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Search the <strong>AFL-CIO Union Finder</strong> to locate unions in your industry</li>
          <li>Attend union meetings and volunteer for organizing committees</li>
          <li>Take union steward training to help enforce your contract</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://aflcio.org/formaunion" target="_blank" rel="noopener">AFL-CIO — Form a Union</a></li>
          <li><a href="https://www.labornotes.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Labor Notes</a> — Training and resources for rank-and-file organizing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#128736;</div>
      <h4>Organize Your Workplace</h4>
      <p>No union at your job? You can start one. Workplace organizing follows proven methods—learn them and apply them carefully.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Identify workplace issues and coworkers who share concerns (start with 1-on-1 conversations)</li>
          <li>Contact <strong>Emergency Workplace Organizing Committee (EWOC)</strong> for free support</li>
          <li>Read <strong>"Secrets of a Successful Organizer"</strong> from Labor Notes</li>
          <li>Build majority support before going public—aim for 70%+ before filing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://workerorganizing.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">EWOC</a> — Free organizing support for any worker</li>
          <li><a href="https://www.nlrb.gov" target="_blank" rel="noopener">NLRB</a> — Know your legal rights</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#127919;</div>
      <h4>Support Union Campaigns</h4>
      <p>Even if you can't unionize your own workplace, you can support workers who are organizing elsewhere.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Honor picket lines and boycotts—never cross</li>
          <li>Donate to strike funds during labor actions</li>
          <li>Write letters of support to workers facing union-busting</li>
          <li>Attend solidarity rallies and share campaigns on social media</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://aflcio.org/strike-map" target="_blank" rel="noopener">AFL-CIO Strike Map</a> — Find active labor actions</li>
          <li><a href="https://www.gofundme.com/c/act/labor-rights" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Labor Strike Funds</a> — Support striking workers</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

<div id="recruiting" class="content-section">
  <h2>Recruiting</h2>
  <p>Movements grow one conversation at a time. Effective recruitment isn't about winning arguments—it's about listening, finding common ground, and inviting people into action.</p>

  <div class="action-grid">
    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#128172;</div>
      <h4>Start with Relationships</h4>
      <p>The most effective recruitment happens through existing relationships. People join movements because someone they trust invited them.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Best Practices:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Listen first:</strong> Ask about their concerns before sharing your views</li>
          <li><strong>Find shared values:</strong> Most people want fairness, security, and dignity</li>
          <li><strong>Make concrete asks:</strong> "Will you come to this meeting?" is better than "You should care about this"</li>
          <li><strong>Follow up:</strong> Recruitment is a process, not a single conversation</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Recommended Reading:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>"No Shortcuts"</strong> by Jane McAlevey — Essential guide to deep organizing</li>
          <li><strong>"Rules for Revolutionaries"</strong> by Becky Bond & Zack Exley</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#127793;</div>
      <h4>Grow Through Organizations</h4>
      <p>Join existing organizations to find community and multiply your impact. Collective action is more effective than individual effort.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Organizations to Consider:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Democratic Socialists of America (DSA)</strong> — Largest socialist org in the US, with local chapters</li>
          <li><strong>Local mutual aid networks</strong> — Search "[your city] mutual aid"</li>
          <li><strong>Tenant unions</strong> — Housing organizing in your area</li>
          <li><strong>Faith-based organizing</strong> — Groups like <strong>PICO</strong> or <strong>IAF</strong> affiliates</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://www.dsausa.org/chapters" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Find a DSA Chapter</a></li>
          <li><a href="https://www.tenantstogether.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Tenants Together</a> — Housing organizing resources</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="warning-box" style="margin-top: 1.5rem;">
    <strong>Important:</strong> Effective recruiting meets people where they are. Avoid jargon, don't lecture, and remember that material conditions—not correct theory—radicalize people. Focus on concrete issues that affect their lives.
  </div>
</div>

<div id="fundraising" class="content-section">
  <h2>Fundraising</h2>
  <p>Money enables action. From printing flyers to supporting full-time organizers, sustainable funding makes movements possible. Fundraising is a skill that can be learned.</p>

  <div class="action-grid">
    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#128176;</div>
      <h4>Individual Donations</h4>
      <p>Small-dollar donations from many people create sustainable, accountable funding that doesn't depend on wealthy benefactors.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Strategies:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Recurring donations:</strong> $5/month from 100 people = $6,000/year</li>
          <li><strong>Peer-to-peer fundraising:</strong> Personal asks outperform mass appeals</li>
          <li><strong>Transparency:</strong> Show exactly how funds are used</li>
          <li><strong>Platforms:</strong> Use <strong>ActBlue</strong> (progressive), <strong>Open Collective</strong> (transparent), or <strong>Patreon</strong> (ongoing support)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Platforms:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://secure.actblue.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ActBlue</a> — Progressive fundraising</li>
          <li><a href="https://opencollective.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Open Collective</a> — Transparent fiscal hosting</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#127881;</div>
      <h4>Events & Community Fundraising</h4>
      <p>Events build community while raising funds. They're also opportunities to recruit and educate.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Event Ideas:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Benefit concerts/shows</strong> — Partner with local artists</li>
          <li><strong>Dinners and potlucks</strong> — Low-cost, high-community events</li>
          <li><strong>Skill-share workshops</strong> — Charge sliding-scale fees</li>
          <li><strong>Merchandise</strong> — T-shirts, buttons, zines (support local printers)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Tips:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li>Partner with local venues for free or discounted space</li>
          <li>Always have a sign-up sheet for future contact</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div id="local-community" class="content-section">
  <h2>Local Community Support</h2>
  <p>Change starts where you live. Building strong local communities creates the foundation for broader transformation and provides immediate material support to neighbors in need.</p>

  <div class="action-grid">
    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#127968;</div>
      <h4>Know Your Neighbors</h4>
      <p>Strong communities are built on relationships. Knowing your neighbors enables mutual aid, collective action, and resilience during crises.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Introduce yourself to neighbors you don't know</li>
          <li>Organize block parties, cleanups, or community gardens</li>
          <li>Create communication channels (group chats, email lists)</li>
          <li>Map community skills and resources—who can fix things? Who has tools?</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://www.shareable.net" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Shareable</a> — Community sharing resources</li>
          <li><strong>"Building Communities from the Inside Out"</strong> by Kretzmann & McKnight</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#127806;</div>
      <h4>Food Security Initiatives</h4>
      <p>Food is a basic need. Supporting local food security helps neighbors while building alternative systems outside capitalist markets.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Volunteer at local <strong>food banks</strong> and <strong>food pantries</strong></li>
          <li>Support <strong>community gardens</strong> and urban farms</li>
          <li>Join or start a <strong>Food Not Bombs</strong> chapter</li>
          <li>Participate in <strong>community fridges</strong> and <strong>little free pantries</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://foodnotbombs.net" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Food Not Bombs</a> — Free food sharing</li>
          <li><a href="https://www.feedingamerica.org/find-your-local-foodbank" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Feeding America</a> — Find local food banks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#128657;</div>
      <h4>Tenant Organizing</h4>
      <p>Housing is the largest expense for most families. Tenant organizing builds power to fight rent increases, evictions, and unsafe conditions.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Talk to neighbors about shared housing issues</li>
          <li>Research tenant rights in your jurisdiction</li>
          <li>Form or join a <strong>tenant union</strong> in your building/neighborhood</li>
          <li>Support <strong>rent control</strong> and <strong>eviction protection</strong> campaigns</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://atun-rsia.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Autonomous Tenant Union Network</a></li>
          <li><a href="https://www.righttothecity.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Right to the City Alliance</a></li>
        </ul>

<div id="homeless-outreach" class="content-section">
  <h2>Homeless Outreach</h2>
  <p>Houselessness is a policy choice, not a personal failing. While we work for systemic solutions, we can provide immediate support and treat unhoused neighbors with dignity and respect.</p>

  <div class="highlight-box">
    <h4>Approach with Respect</h4>
    <p>Unhoused people are <strong>people first</strong>—with agency, preferences, and dignity. Ask what they need rather than assuming. Build ongoing relationships rather than one-time charity. Support organizing efforts led by homeless people themselves.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="action-grid">
    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#128230;</div>
      <h4>Direct Support</h4>
      <p>Providing immediate material support to unhoused neighbors.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Care kits:</strong> Socks, hygiene products, water, snacks, hand warmers (ask what's needed)</li>
          <li><strong>Know local resources:</strong> Shelters, warming centers, meal programs, showers</li>
          <li><strong>Cash:</strong> The most flexible form of help—trust people to know their needs</li>
          <li><strong>Harm reduction supplies:</strong> Narcan, safer use supplies (where legal)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Resources:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://www.hudexchange.info/housing-and-homeless-assistance/homeless-help/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">HUD Homeless Resources</a></li>
          <li><a href="https://www.samhsa.gov/find-help/national-helpline" target="_blank" rel="noopener">SAMHSA National Helpline</a> — Mental health/substance abuse</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#128587;</div>
      <h4>Advocacy & Systemic Change</h4>
      <p>Direct support helps individuals; systemic change helps everyone. Both are necessary.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Steps:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Support <strong>Housing First</strong> policies—housing without preconditions works</li>
          <li>Oppose <strong>hostile architecture</strong> and anti-homeless ordinances</li>
          <li>Advocate for <strong>permanent supportive housing</strong> funding</li>
          <li>Support homeless-led organizations like <strong>National Homeless Union</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Organizations:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://nationalhomeless.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">National Coalition for the Homeless</a></li>
          <li><a href="https://www.streetroots.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Street Roots</a> — Homeless-run newspaper/organizing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

<div id="red-aid" class="content-section">
  <h2>Red Aid</h2>
  <p>Red Aid refers to mutual aid and legal support networks rooted in leftist solidarity traditions. The concept originated with International Red Aid (MOPR) in the 1920s, supporting political prisoners and activists facing repression.</p>

  <div class="highlight-box">
    <h4>What is Red Aid?</h4>
    <p>Red Aid organizations provide legal defense, bail funds, and material support to activists arrested or persecuted for political activity. Modern incarnations include legal observer programs, jail support, and solidarity networks that ensure no one faces state repression alone.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="action-grid">
    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#9878;</div>
      <h4>Legal Support Networks</h4>
      <p>Know your rights and support those who face legal consequences for activism.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Ways to Participate:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Train as a <strong>legal observer</strong> with the National Lawyers Guild</li>
          <li>Donate to <strong>bail funds</strong> in your area</li>
          <li>Provide <strong>jail support</strong>—waiting outside with food, rides, and comfort for released activists</li>
          <li>Know your rights: Don't talk to police without a lawyer</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Organizations:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://www.nlg.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">National Lawyers Guild</a> — Legal observer training, legal defense</li>
          <li><a href="https://www.communityjusticeexchange.org/nbfn-directory" target="_blank" rel="noopener">National Bail Fund Network</a> — Find local bail funds</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="action-card">
      <div class="action-icon">&#129309;</div>
      <h4>Solidarity & Mutual Aid</h4>
      <p>Red Aid extends beyond legal support to encompass all forms of solidarity with those targeted for their activism.</p>
      <div class="action-steps">
        <h5>Concrete Actions:</h5>
        <ul>
          <li>Support <strong>political prisoner</strong> letter-writing campaigns</li>
          <li>Contribute to <strong>commissary funds</strong> for incarcerated activists</li>
          <li>Attend court dates in solidarity (court support)</li>
          <li>Help with childcare, meals, or bills for activists facing legal troubles</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="resource-box">
        <strong>Key Organizations:</strong>
        <ul>
          <li><a href="https://www.abcf.net" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Anarchist Black Cross Federation</a> — Political prisoner support</li>
          <li><a href="https://www.thejerichomovement.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Jericho Movement</a> — Political prisoner advocacy</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="warning-box" style="margin-top: 1.5rem;">
    <strong>Security Culture Reminder:</strong> When supporting activists facing legal issues, practice good security culture. Don't discuss details of cases publicly, don't share information about others' activities without consent, and assume surveillance. Solidarity means protecting each other.
  </div>
</div>
